> The three big ones in life are wealth, health, and happiness. We pursue them in that order, but their importance is in the reverse.

Be a paradox. Lift weights. Read books. Be ruthless in business. Be relaxed in life. Attract a strong partner, but stand complete on your own. Build empires while staying grounded in your roots. Surround yourself with winners, but never follow the crowd.

The older you get, the more you realize most people do not want truth. They want a version of reality that protects their ego, excuses their weakness, and makes their envy feel moral. A serious person stops trying to wake everyone up and builds quietly while they defend their sleep.

## Core Truths

- Love needs action.
- Trust needs proof.
- Sorry needs change.

> Be quiet, work hard, and stay healthy. It is not ambition or skill that will set you apart, but sanity.

Everything is an opportunity for excellence. The impediment to action advances action. What stands in the way becomes the way.

## Foundational Principles

- Thoughts are not true.
- Feelings do not require actions.
- Things are not good or bad. They simply are.
- Our greatest enemy is ignorance.
- To change your life, change your surroundings.
- Our actions, not our past, define who we are.

> In serenity, maintain vigilance. In turmoil, uphold tranquility.

## Character

Be a pleasure to deal with:

- Kind when others are not.
- Calm when things go sideways.
- Reliable under pressure.

Intelligence alone is overrated when it comes without tact, timing, or emotional range.

Be someone who lightens the load, not someone who quietly adds to it. People remember who brought clarity in chaos, who did not make everything about themselves, and who made hard moments feel a little less heavy.

## Think Like A Farmer

- Do not shout at the crops.
- Do not blame the crop for not growing fast enough.
- Do not uproot crops before they have had a chance to grow.
- Choose the best plants for the soil.
- Irrigate and fertilize.
- Remove weeds.
- Remember that there will be good seasons and bad seasons. You cannot control the weather, only prepare for it.

## On Wisdom

- You cannot learn what you think you already know.
- Avoiding stupidity is easier than seeking brilliance.
- What you are doing right now is a hypothesis to be tested, not an ideology to be proved.

## On Action

> Go big. You either get an epic story or an epic outcome. When you die, you will be happy with either.

- Amateurs have a goal. Professionals have a system.
- What do I care about how many people read my books in one hundred years? What matters is whether I am doing my best right now.

## On Responsibility

- Whoever blames others has a long way to go on the journey.
- Whoever blames themselves is halfway there.
- Whoever blames no one has arrived.

## On Perception

- Preoccupied with a single leaf, you will not see the tree.
- Preoccupied with a single tree, you will miss the entire forest.
- Your perception of me is a reflection of you. My reaction to you is an awareness of me.

## On Change

- Everyone tries to change other people while wanting to be accepted as they are.
- The better path is to change yourself and accept other people as they are.

## Timeless Quotes

> You can be invincible, as long as you do not enter any fight where victory does not depend on you. - Epictetus

> The degree to which a person can grow is directly proportional to the amount of truth they can accept about themselves without running away. - Leland Val Van De Wall

> Right is right even if nobody does it. Wrong is wrong even if everybody does it. - Augustine of Hippo

> It does not make sense to continue wanting something if you are not willing to do what it takes to get it. If you do not want to live the lifestyle, release yourself from the desire. - James Clear

Freedom is knowing what you must do.

When you miss a turn, your GPS does not judge you. It recalculates. No matter how many detours you take, it finds another way forward. Life works like that too. You will make mistakes, but your destination does not vanish. The route changes.

Beliefs are not possible if knowledge is not possible, and knowledge is not possible if the immaterial world does not exist. This world is necessarily unified, and all the parts are internally related in relation to the Good or One. - Lloyd P. Gerson

We become what we contemplate.
