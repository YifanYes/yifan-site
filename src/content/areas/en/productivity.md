> Productivity is about getting the results you want, as soon as you can, with the least amount of effort.

Stop measuring days by degree of productivity and start experiencing them by degree of presence.

> What is the simplest action I can take today that would create forward progress?

> Only a slave quantifies existence through productivity.

## Efficiency Vs Effectiveness

The most efficient use of your time is to do what you do best and what no one else can do.

- **Effectiveness** - Doing the right things and getting the result you intend.
- **Efficiency** - Doing things right with minimal waste of time and effort.

To achieve more, you must be both effective and efficient, but effectiveness should come first.

> In the long run, prioritization beats efficiency.

## Tools

- **Calendar** - Save event reminders and time blocks. Establish daily focus.
- **Second brain** - Capture actionable ideas that can advance projects.

## Making The Most Of Evenings

The brain works in roughly 90-minute focus blocks. Structure evenings with intention:

- **Recovery** - Decompression time. No decisions. No planning. Just recharge.
- **Growth** - Learn something new, move, or work on a hobby.
- **Connection** - Spend quality time with family and friends.

> Your evening affects your morning more than your morning affects your evening.

## Attention Residue

Dr. Sophie Leroy defines attention residue as the persistence of cognitive activity about task A even after one has stopped working on task A and started performing task B.

There is a cognitive switching cost to shifting attention. When your attention shifts, residue remains with the prior task and impairs cognitive performance on the new task.

## Goals And Objectives

### Outcome Goals

- Final target.
- Long term.
- Not fully under your control.

### Performance Goals

- Short or medium term.
- Fully under your control.
- Their satisfaction helps you achieve outcome goals.

### Backward Goal Setting

1. Visualize the end goal in detail.
2. Set milestones working backward.
3. Identify steps for each milestone.
4. Allocate the necessary resources.
5. Assign timeframes based on the end goal.
6. Start with the nearest step.
7. Stay flexible and adjust when needed.
8. Review the plan regularly for alignment.

## ABCDE Method

List the tasks you need to do, then categorize them:

- **A** - Most important.
- **B** - Important.
- **C** - Nice to do.
- **D** - Delegate.
- **E** - Eliminate.

Focus on A tasks until they are completed. Review and adjust the list regularly.

## Productivity Review Questions

- What do I do that I think is productive but is not?
- What do I do that I do not think is productive but actually is?
- What am I not achieving simply because I have not asked?
- What can I do today that was not possible six months ago?

## Quick Techniques

### Two-Minute Rule

If a task takes less than two minutes to complete, do it right away.

### Five-Second Rule

Count backward from five to one, then force yourself to take action.

### One-Three-Five Rule

At the start of each day, identify:

- **1** big thing to accomplish.
- **3** medium things.
- **5** small tasks.

### Pomodoro Technique

1. Set a timer for 25 minutes and focus on one task.
2. After the timer rings, take a five-minute break.
3. Repeat this cycle four times, then take a 20- to 30-minute break.

### Eat The Frog

Tackle your most challenging task first thing in the morning.

### Audit Tasks

Automate, eliminate, delegate.

## Key Insights

> Preparation always beats planning. Planning is based on the expectation of order. Preparation is based on the expectation of chaos.

- People with high levels of self-control are good at avoiding temptation, not merely resisting it.
- Busy is not productive. Apply effort deliberately.
- Strategy is the prioritization of limited resources against unlimited options.
- You can only have one first priority.

> Just because it is not optimal does not mean it is not beneficial.

### Zeigarnik Effect

An activity that has been interrupted is more readily recalled.
